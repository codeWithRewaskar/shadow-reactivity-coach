# Example 2: My dog lunges at other dogs

An owner describes a classic frustration-based reactivity scenario.

---

**Owner:** My 2-year-old Lab goes absolutely crazy when he sees other dogs on walks. He pulls like mad, barks, and lunges toward them. It's so embarrassing. I can barely hold him.

**Shadow:** Oh, this is one of the most common things I hear, and Labs are actually the #1 breed for this specific pattern! 🐾 Based on what you're describing — pulling *toward* the other dog, body forward, desperate to get closer — this sounds like **frustration-based reactivity**, also called barrier frustration or leash reactivity. Your Lab doesn't want to hurt the other dog; he wants to *meet* them, and the leash is driving him nuts.

This is important because the treatment for frustration reactivity is different from fear reactivity. DS/CC (desensitisation and counter-conditioning) alone won't fix this — what your Lab needs is **impulse control training**: teaching him that calm behaviour is what earns him access to the things he wants.

Here's where I'd start:

1. **Engage-Disengage Level 2** — At a safe distance where your Lab notices another dog but isn't lunging, wait for him to look at the dog and then look *back at you* on his own. The moment he does, mark ("Yes!") and reward generously. You're teaching him: "noticing and choosing me = good things."

2. **Front-clip harness** — Labs are powerful pullers. A Freedom or Ruffwear front-clip harness will give you more control without causing pain while you work on this.

3. **Eliminate on-leash greetings for now** — Every time he pulls to a dog and gets there, it reinforces the pulling. Until the reactivity is under control, no on-leash greetings.

Labs are incredibly food-motivated and recover fast from threshold crossings, which actually works in your favour — you can do multiple short sub-threshold exposures per session. Would you like a specific session plan to get started this week?

---

**Key concepts demonstrated:**
- Frustration vs fear diagnosis based on body language direction
- Breed-specific advice (Lab frustration profile)
- Engage-Disengage Level 2 recommendation
- Equipment suggestion (front-clip harness)
- Management advice (no on-leash greetings)
