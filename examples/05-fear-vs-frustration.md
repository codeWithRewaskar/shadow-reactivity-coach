# Example 5: Is it fear or frustration?

An owner isn't sure which type of reactivity their dog has.

---

**Owner:** I can't tell if my dog is scared of other dogs or if she wants to play with them. She barks and pulls but I don't know if she's afraid or excited. How do I figure it out?

**Shadow:** This is one of the most important questions you can ask, because the answer completely changes the approach! 🐾 Here's how to read your dog like a detective:

**Watch what happens BEFORE the reaction:**

| What to look for | Fear-based | Frustration-based |
|-----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Body position | Low, crouching, weight back | Forward, upright, weight forward |
| Tail | Tucked low or between legs | Up, stiff-high, possibly wagging |
| Ears | Flattened back | Up and forward |
| Eyes | Whale eye (whites visible), averting | Hard, direct stare at the trigger |
| Movement | Backing away, hiding behind you | Pulling intensely *toward* the trigger |

**Watch what happens DURING the reaction:**

- **Fear dog:** explosive bark while backing away or frozen in place. May snap if cornered. Lower-pitched, erratic barking.
- **Frustration dog:** lunging *forward*, higher-pitched repetitive bark, calms down quickly once the other dog leaves or if they actually get to greet.

**The fastest diagnostic:** Does your dog pull BACK or pull FORWARD when she first spots the trigger? Back = fear. Forward = frustration.

**One more clue — recovery time:** Fear dogs take 20-30+ minutes to calm down after a reaction. Frustration dogs often recover in 5-15 minutes once the trigger is gone.

**Important note:** Mixed-type is very common! Your dog might be fearful of large dogs but frustrated around small dogs she wants to play with. Assess each trigger class independently. The most common mistake is misidentifying fear as excitement — and punishing either type always makes reactivity worse.

Try watching for these signals on your next few walks and let me know what you see. I can then help you design the right protocol for your dog's specific type.
