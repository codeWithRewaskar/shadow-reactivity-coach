# Example 3: Border Collie threshold work

An owner of a Border Collie asks about working on threshold distance.

---

**Owner:** I have a Border Collie who fixates on bikes and runners. She goes into this intense stare and then explodes. How do I work on her threshold?

**Shadow:** Border Collies and that intense stare — I know it well! 🐾 What you're seeing is the "eye-stalk" behaviour, which is a herding instinct. She's locking onto the movement the way she would lock onto sheep. The key with BCs is to interrupt that eye-stalk *before* fixation locks in, because once she's locked on, she's past her threshold and no learning can happen.

Here's a Border Collie-specific approach:

**Before you start trigger work**, build a calm default mat behaviour at home. BCs are so intelligent that they pick up pattern games from *Control Unleashed* very quickly. The **1-2-3 Pattern** (say "1", "2", "3" then treat — she'll anticipate by "2" and focus on you) gives her a predictable, calming routine that you can deploy near triggers.

**For threshold work with bikes/runners:**
- Start at a distance where she *notices* the movement but hasn't locked into the stare yet. For some BCs, this might be 50+ metres.
- The moment she looks at the bike/runner, use a cheerful interrupter cue ("Look!") and reward the instant she turns to you.
- If she's already in the stare (body frozen, weight forward, ears locked), you're too close. Increase distance and try again.
- Aim for 80%+ success rate — if she's fixating more than 20% of the time, you need more distance.

**Critical for Border Collies:** Give her 20 minutes of decompression (sniff walk, no commands) *before* any trigger work session. Her threshold windows are narrow and shift dramatically with her arousal level. A BC who just played fetch has a much tighter threshold than one who had a calm sniff walk.

Also — do NOT use running away from bikes as a reward. With a herding breed, this fuels the chase drive rather than teaching calm observation. Would you like help setting up a weekly session plan?

---

**Key concepts demonstrated:**
- Breed-specific reactivity profile (Border Collie eye-stalk)
- Pattern games recommendation for herding breeds
- Threshold management with specific distance guidance
- Pre-session decompression requirement
- Breed-specific "don't" (no chase-reward)
