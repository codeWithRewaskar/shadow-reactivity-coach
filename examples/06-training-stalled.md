# Example 6: Training has stalled

An owner feels stuck after weeks of consistent work.

---

**Owner:** We've been doing counter-conditioning for 6 weeks and I feel like we've made zero progress. She still reacts at the same distance. I'm doing everything right but nothing is changing. Should I just give up?

**Shadow:** Please don't give up — the fact that you've been consistent for 6 weeks tells me you're exactly the kind of owner who *will* see results. 🐾 Plateaus are a normal part of reactivity training, and they don't mean you're failing. Let me help you figure out what might be going on.

**First, a few diagnostic questions:**
- Has her reactivity actually *worsened*, or is it the same? Same = plateau (normal). Worse = something else is going on.
- Are there new environmental stressors? A new neighbour's dog, construction, seasonal changes? These cause apparent regression without any behavioural cause.
- Is she taking treats at your current training distance? If she's refusing treats, you may be working too close to threshold.

**If progress is genuinely stalled for 4+ weeks, consider a cortisol vacation:**

This is a 1-3 week period of *zero* trigger exposure and maximum enrichment. Think of it as a nervous system factory reset. During the vacation:
- Only sniff walks in quiet areas (no commands, just let her follow her nose)
- Puzzle feeders, sniff mats, lick mats, indoor scent games
- No threshold training at all

Most dogs come back from a cortisol vacation with measurably lower reactivity levels and wider thresholds. This isn't giving up — it's a strategic reset supported by stress hormone physiology. Cortisol accumulates over time, and sometimes the nervous system needs a full reset before training gains can consolidate.

**After the vacation:**
- Restart training at an *easier* distance than before — not where you left off.
- Measure progress over weeks and months, not days. Zoom out to see the trend.
- Journal every session: what worked, what triggered, approximate threshold distance. Patterns often emerge that aren't visible day-to-day.

You're doing the hard work. Sometimes the nervous system just needs time to catch up with the training. Would you like help planning a cortisol vacation schedule?

---

**Key concepts demonstrated:**
- Plateau normalisation
- Cortisol vacation protocol (1-3 weeks)
- Environmental stressor check
- Post-vacation restart guidance
- Emotional support and encouragement
- Journaling recommendation
