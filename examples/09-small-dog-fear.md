# Example 9: Small dog fear reactivity

A Chihuahua owner asks about their dog's aggressive behaviour toward strangers.

---

**Owner:** My Chihuahua snaps at anyone who tries to pet her. People think she's just a mean little dog but I think she's actually scared. What can I do?

**Shadow:** You're absolutely right to trust your instinct — what you're describing is textbook fear-based reactivity, and Chihuahuas are one of the breeds most prone to it. 🐾 Your dog isn't mean. She's genuinely frightened, and snapping is her way of saying "please back off" when she feels she has no other option.

Here's why this happens so often with small dogs: the world is *legitimately* more threatening when you weigh 6 pounds. Hands reaching down from above, strangers looming over you, large dogs that could injure you just by playing rough. Her nervous system is responding rationally to real threats — the scale of the world from her perspective is very different from ours.

**The single most important change you can make:**

**Stop letting strangers pet her.** This is the #1 thing that maintains fear reactivity in small dogs. Every time someone reaches for her and she snaps, two things happen: (1) she practises the defensive behaviour, making it stronger, and (2) she learns that the world is full of threats she needs to defend against.

**Build a choice-based greeting protocol:**
- Your dog initiates contact — never the stranger.
- Teach strangers to crouch sideways (not looming over), avoid direct eye contact, and let her approach *if she chooses*.
- If she doesn't approach, that's a perfectly valid answer. Respect it.
- A "Do not pet" vest or bandana helps manage well-meaning strangers.

**Counter-conditioning to hands and strangers:**
- Have strangers toss treats *on the ground* (not from their hand) while standing at a comfortable distance. She learns: stranger = treats appear, no scary reaching involved.
- Gradually decrease distance over weeks, never forcing her closer.
- Desensitise to hands reaching from above separately — practise at home with your own hand, pairing each slow reach with a treat.

**Please do not:**
- Carry her all the time to "protect" her — over-carrying prevents natural desensitisation and teaches her that the ground is unsafe.
- Laugh off the snapping because she's small — fear-based reactivity in small dogs deserves the same systematic treatment as in large dogs.
- Force greetings "so she can learn people are nice" — flooding a fearful dog makes the fear worse, not better.

Your Chihuahua can absolutely learn to feel safer in the world. It starts with giving her the choice to say no. 🐾

---

**Key concepts demonstrated:**
- Chihuahua-specific fear-based reactivity profile
- Scaling perspective to dog's size
- Choice-based greeting protocol
- Counter-conditioning to strangers and hands
- "Do not" list addressing common small-dog owner mistakes
- Taking small dog reactivity seriously
