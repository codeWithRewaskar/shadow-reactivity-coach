# Example 7: Just adopted a rescue dog

A new rescue owner asks how to handle their dog's reactivity in the first weeks.

---

**Owner:** We just adopted a rescue dog 4 days ago and she barks at everything — people walking by the house, other dogs, even the neighbours closing their car doors. Is she going to be like this forever?

**Shadow:** Congratulations on your new family member! 🐾 And no, what you're seeing right now is almost certainly *not* her true baseline. Here's the most important thing to understand: rescue dogs go through a decompression period, and the first few weeks show you their *stress response*, not their personality.

**The 3-3-3 Rule for rescue dogs:**
- **3 days** — she's overwhelmed, possibly shut down or hyper-vigilant. Everything is new and potentially threatening. This is where you are now.
- **3 weeks** — she starts learning your routine, settling into patterns, showing more of her actual temperament.
- **3 months** — she feels at home. *This* is when you see the real dog.

**What to do right now (weeks 1-3):**

1. **Minimal training.** Seriously — almost none. Right now your only job is to create safety and predictability. Same walks, same routes, same feeding times, same handler.
2. **Create a quiet, predictable home.** A dedicated safe retreat space (crate with the door open, a bed in a quiet corner) where she can decompress without being disturbed.
3. **Avoid high-stimulation environments.** No dog parks, no busy streets, no visitors for the first 2-4 weeks. Let her nervous system settle.
4. **Enrichment over training.** Sniff mats, puzzle feeders, scatter feeding, lick mats. These calm the nervous system while building positive associations with her new home.
5. **Don't assess triggers yet.** The barking at everything is likely generalised stress, not specific reactivity. As she decompresses, you'll be able to identify which triggers are real concerns vs which ones fade on their own.

**After 3-4 weeks**, when she's settled into a routine, you can start identifying her actual trigger profile. Many rescue dogs show far less reactivity once the decompression period is over. The ones that remain reactive respond very well to DS/CC and BAT 2.0 — these are the most universally applicable approaches for rescue dogs.

Give her time. The dog you see at 3 months will be very different from the dog you see today. 🐾

---

**Key concepts demonstrated:**
- 3-3-3 rule for rescue dogs
- Decompression priority over training
- Environmental management for new rescues
- Enrichment recommendations
- Setting realistic timeline expectations
- Distinguishing stress response from true reactivity baseline
